Solving Maxwell's Equations

My current research focuses on solving Maxwell’s equations in three-dimensional space to simulate the propagation of ultrashort laser pulses and their interactions with nanostructures, particularly metallic and dielectric nanowires. Using Pseudospectral Time Domain (PSTD) and Finite-Difference Time-Domain (FDTD) methods, I model nonlinear, time-resolved electromagnetic fields in nanoscale environments. My responsibilities have included setting up custom simulations, implementing boundary conditions, adjusting solver parameters, and generating meaningful visualizations. The broader goal is to better understand femtosecond-scale light-matter interactions—knowledge that has implications for optical computing, nano-fabrication, and photonic device engineering. This work has strengthened both my coding abilities and my physical intuition for complex electromagnetic systems. Using PSTD to solve Maxwell's equations in a 3D computational space, including absorbing boundary conditions such as CPML and injecting a light source with total field scatter field, developed in Fortran and visualized with Igor Pro 8.
