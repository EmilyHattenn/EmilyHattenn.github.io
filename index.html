Solving Maxwell's Equations with PSTD/FDTD

My current research focuses on solving Maxwell's equations in three-dimensional space to simulate the propagation of ultrashort laser pulses and their interactions with nanostructures, particularly metallic and dielectric nanowires. Using Pseudospectral Time Domain (PSTD) and Finite-Difference Time-Domain (FDTD) methods, I model nonlinear, time-resolved electromagnetic fields in nanoscale environments.

My responsibilities include setting up custom simulations, implementing absorbing boundary conditions (CPML), configuring total field scatter field injection, and generating meaningful visualizations. This work has strengthened both my coding abilities and my physical intuition for complex electromagnetic systems, with implications for optical computing, nano-fabrication, and photonic device engineering.
